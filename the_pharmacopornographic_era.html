Pharmacologic

At the end of 40s the notion of hormone has become a subject of scientific inquiry. By the end of 40s first molecules of progesterone and estrogen were extracted from the urine of pregnant women. Shortly after that the first synthetic hormone, Norethindrone, was commercialised. Harold Gilles, a UK surgeon, through 13 phalloplastic surgeries and testosterone administration protocol to affirm masculinisation, successfully completes the first female-to-male transition on Michael Dillon. The first openly discussed male-to-female transition of a US Soldier George W. Jorgensen to Christine is sucessfully completed in 1952.

First contraceptive pill was invented in 1957 and approved four years later. The Pill has become the first biochemical method of distinction between heterosexual pleasure from reproduction, forever changing the discourse of heterosexuality.
