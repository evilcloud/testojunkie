A self-depreciating attempt to cope with a death of a friend. The coping turns into a ritualised act of group therapy run by a bunch of anonymous disenfranchised people acting out on their gender dysphoria.

A subject of surveillance is touched, but no substantial commitment was made on the topic, aside from implication of it being the case as the consequence of her/his continuous act of exhibitionism.

Surveillance → exhibitionism
Sorrow → change

The narrative

The initial character settings is a bit confusing:

  • the book (or chapter, rather) is an memoir (the reliability of the narrative is a huge question, raised by the author himself)
  • the trigger of the events, including the book, is the death of William, a friend deeply admired by the author.
  • the author is participating in a group of like-minded individuals who transform their bodies into the opposite sex by the use of grey market pharmaceuticals and questionable rites
